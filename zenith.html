Zenith Content

Basics

The 'Zenith' label specifically refers to a type of monster introduced in Frontier Z and any equipment or skills that are solely available from doing these types of monsters.

Zenith Monsters themselves are content that is scaled to start harder than the existing Exotic monsters (G Rank Nargacuga, Zinogre, etc.) at GR200 and eventually at GR800 to be the hardest standard content in general, only surpassed by the super hard event quests such as Howling Zinogre.

Zenith equipment is pretty much a flat upgrade over all standard G equipment (Raviente weapons excluded) and has higher defense, true raw, better skill points and gives one of the new Zenith Skills per piece, cuff or weapon equipped.

Zenith Skills are always on skills mostly designed to buff existing skills or to allow you to simply get more skills in general on a set by expanding the number of skill slots available. For example the Zenith Daimyo Hermitaur Headpiece will give you the Quake Res Up Zenith Skill which simply increase your level of Quake Res by 1 tier, this includes in combination skills such as Three Worlds Protection and allows the skill to be upgraded beyond Quake Res+2 to become what is functionally Quake Res+3. These additional tiers of resistance skills are required to counter Extreme Wind Pressure, Roars and Quakes.

As mentioned above, Zeniths will commonly have levels of Roars, Quake and Wind above that which can be countered by standard Super High-Grade Earplugs and similar. The only way to counter this is to either get a piece of Zenith equipment which counters them or to simply actively evade being hit by the effects. Similarly Poison, Paralysis, Sleep, Fire Blight etc. all get increases which can be countered by either having high enough resistance to the appropriate element or by having a Zenith Skill to boost the standard resistances. It is worth noting that not all statuses can be nullified. You can never counter Extreme Poison and as such should simply try to never be hit by it.

Zenith Monsters
Basics


Zenith Monsters are variants of existing monsters that possess extremely powerful attacks and enhanced abilities such as Ultra Wind Pressure, Roars, Tremor, Sleep, Poison and Paralysis. All Zenith monsters are notably different from their standard counterpart in that they have a single highly developed part which - when broken - gives a specific material type depending on the rank.

There are four different ratings for Zenith monsters, ★1 through ★4, each of these levels increases the monster's Attack and Effective Health Pool. Every single version of a Zenith gives a defense reduction of 1050, the same as that from Exotic Monster quests.

Everything else can be farmed on the GR600 versions without issue. See below for more elaboration on this point.

All zeniths have a specific extra developed body part. If you manage to break this part you will get a special 100% break item, this item has four variants that are all 100% within a certain rank band.

Breaking a part usually reduces the threat of the monster, this varies between monsters with some being crippled moveset wise and losing properties such as Zenith level Roar, Tremor or Wind Pressure while others only slightly ease the difficulty of the monster.

From this you can see that although it may take longer you could quite comfortably farm the vast majority of items from simply doing mostly ★1 to ★3 versions of the monster and would only actually need to ever fight 3 of the GR800 ★4 variant as long as you consistently broke the horn.

As there are no decorations or actual benefits to getting ZX armour to Lv7 you can simply leave it at Lv1 without missing anything beyond some defense. Completing the actual ZX upgrades is not terribly recommendable as it only actually adds around 30-50 defense while using around 70% of the total items that are needed for simply reaching ZX Lv1.

As you can see it is a rather large amount of materials to simply get extra defense. ZX Lv2 through ZX Lv7 requires 142 materials total, in comparison for Z through ZX Lv1 you need 196 materials total. It is almost always going to be better to get other armour pieces or to work on weapons over upgrading to ZX Lv7.

New Blights

All of the new blights inflicted by Zenith Monsters can be countered by having Appropriate Resistance values at 55 or higher. The easiest way to achieve this is to use both the SR All Res Up (+14~19) and individual Res Up (+25~34) skills and Guild Food (+30) to maximise your resistance. This can result in up to 83 in the individual resistances which should counter the blights on almost any set that might be in use.

Outside of the blights you can also buy Cancellation Fruit (打消果實) on the Pink Store NPC, this instantly cures any blights you might be inflicted with as long as they have not rendered you incapable of using them.

Zenith Skills

Zenith Skills are similar in function to Burst, Origin or Socketed Tower Weapon skills but with a key difference, they are mostly used to enhance other skills or simply enable you to get more skills in general.

For example an Armour Piece may have Guard Up, this makes it so while using any level of the skill Guard you would have it increased by one level, with the equivalent of Guard+3 or Zenith Guard giving new unique effects.

Zenith Armour


Zenith Armour has four levels, Z, ZF, ZY and ZX. Z and ZF only have 1 upgrade each and then ZY and ZX have the typical 7. These upgrades correlate with GR200, GR400, GR600 and GR800 tiers of the various Zenith monsters. All ZX armour eclipses standard GX or GP Armours and typically has at least 25 points across 5 different skills on it. On top of this it naturally also has a Zenith Skill associated.

There are Premium and Lottery Zenith Armours that have ZP upgrades with the current sets progression having GP upgrade straight into ZP. They have less defense than a standard ZX piece but more than a normal GX. These globally require only materials from a GR200 Hypnoc with the Lottery ones also requiring you to have 2 extra tickets for each piece you want to upgrade to ZP.

The skills on Lottery pieces are usually static and unchanged on upgrading to ZP but Premium Kits will get completely overhauled skills to put them in line with the more commonly used skills in the updates that followed their initial release (Fencing replaced with Sword God, etc.).

All Z pieces count towards the skill slot expansion mechanic for G Rank armour. 5 ZX pieces will allow you to equip 12 skills even if no piece has Skill Slots Up on it innately. Likewise if you had 1 piece with Skill Slots Up this would increase to 13 and so on.

Zenith Cuffs

Zenith Cuffs are fairly straight forward, they all require Zenith Monster materials to be crafted, take two slots and are all rated as SZ meaning they don't need a Premium Outfit. They do not naturally give skill points and instead give an additional Zenith Skill. For example with an SZ Cuff you could buff Thunder Clad or you could add an extra Skill Slot.

These cuffs all take two standard pugi cuff slots meaning you cannot get any skill points from cuffs while also utilising one.

Zenith Weapons

Zenith Weapons can be considered the current standard for power in terms of weapons. They all come with a Zenith skill that varies and can be highly enabling. Zenith Weapons also include a Zenith Partbreaker effect this increases damage dealt to parts by 1.50x, functionally reducing the hits you need to land on a Zenith part to 66% of a normal weapon. Upgrading weapons uses materials from across all tiers with the final upgrade needing ★4 Zenith Breaks, ★4 Common Mats, Semi-Rares and Rares
